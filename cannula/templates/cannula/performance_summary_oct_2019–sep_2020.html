Malaria contribution to OPD attendance in FY20 averaged 46.8% (2,083,260/4,455,049) well above the target of 35%, ranging from 46% in FY20Q1 to 46.8%, 48.3% and 44.6% in FY20Q2, FY20Q3 and FY20Q4, respectively. The geographical factors (vast water bodies) and human economic activities (rice growing and sugar cane growing) that is associated with stagnant water has greatly contributed to the vast mosquito breeding grounds resulting into high malaria burden in the East central region. Only Bugiri (20.8%), Jinja (30.1%) Namutumba (31.1%) Iganga (42.0%) and Mayuge (49.4%) districts had annual average of malaria burden less than 50% of the annual outpatient attendance. The relatively lower burden is probably due to Indoor residual spraying supported by PMI Vector Link in Namutumba and Bugiri districts. Jinja district being more urban than any other district performed better on this indicator probably because individuals in urban setting are more likely to take up malaria prevention measures like use of LLINs coupled with better housing facilities screened from mosquitoes than the rural population.
